Beta Distribution Analysis

Step-by-step derivation of moments, mean, and variance.

Problem Statement

Assume the result from standard advanced calculus that:

$$\int_{0}^{1} u^{\alpha-1}(1 - u)^{\beta-1} du = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha + \beta)}$$

If \( Z \) has a Beta distribution with parameters \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \), find \( E[Z^m(1 - Z)^n] \) for any non-negative integers \( m \) and \( n \). Hence, derive the mean and variance of \( Z \).

Step 1: Finding \( E[Z^m(1 - Z)^n] \)

The Probability Density Function (PDF) of a Beta distribution is defined as:

$$f_Z(z) = \frac{1}{B(\alpha, \beta)} z^{\alpha-1}(1-z)^{\beta-1}, \quad 0 \leq z \leq 1$$

Using the Law of the Unconscious Statistician (LOTUS), we have:

$$E[Z^m(1 - Z)^n] = \int_{0}^{1} z^m(1 - z)^n \left[ \frac{1}{B(\alpha, \beta)} z^{\alpha-1}(1-z)^{\beta-1} \right] dz$$

Combine the terms with common bases:

$$E[Z^m(1 - Z)^n] = \frac{1}{B(\alpha, \beta)} \int_{0}^{1} z^{(\alpha+m)-1}(1-z)^{(\beta+n)-1} dz$$

Note that the integral above is the Beta function \( B(\alpha+m, \beta+n) \).

$$E[Z^m(1 - Z)^n] = \frac{B(\alpha+m, \beta+n)}{B(\alpha, \beta)} = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+m)\Gamma(\beta+n)}{\Gamma(\alpha+m+\beta+n)} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}$$

Step 2: Deriving the Mean \( E[Z] \)

The mean is the expected value where \( m = 1 \) and \( n = 0 \):

$$E[Z] = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+1)\Gamma(\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta+1)} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}$$

Using the recursive property \( \Gamma(x+1) = x\Gamma(x) \):

$$E[Z] = \frac{\alpha\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}{(\alpha+\beta)\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)} = \frac{\alpha}{\alpha + \beta}$$
Result: Mean

\( \mu = \frac{\alpha}{\alpha + \beta} \)

Step 3: Deriving the Variance \( Var(Z) \)

First, we find the second moment \( E[Z^2] \) by setting \( m = 2, n = 0 \):

$$E[Z^2] = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+2)\Gamma(\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta+2)} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)} = \frac{\alpha(\alpha+1)}{(\alpha+\beta)(\alpha+\beta+1)}$$

The variance is calculated as \( Var(Z) = E[Z^2] - (E[Z])^2 \):

$$Var(Z) = \frac{\alpha(\alpha+1)}{(\alpha+\beta)(\alpha+\beta+1)} - \left( \frac{\alpha}{\alpha+\beta} \right)^2$$

After algebraic simplification (finding a common denominator):

$$Var(Z) = \frac{\alpha\beta}{(\alpha+\beta)^2(\alpha+\beta+1)}$$
