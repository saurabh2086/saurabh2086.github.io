This is a classic conditional probability puzzle that shows how additional information can dramatically change an outcome's likelihood.

The Question: Approximately 1/125 of all births are fraternal twins and 1/300 of births are identical twins. Elvis Presley had a twin brother (who died at birth). What is the probability that Elvis was an identical twin?

The probability is 5/11, or approximately 45.5%.

In this problem, we observe the effect (Sex Combination is BB) and use it to infer the probability of the hidden cause (Type of Twinning).

Step 1: Define the Probabilities of Twin Boys

First, let's calculate the chances of any given birth resulting in two boys (BB) for both identical and fraternal twins.

For Identical Twins (I):

  • The probability of a birth being identical is P(I) = 1/300.
  • Identical twins are always the same sex, so the probability of them being two boys is P(BB | I) = 1/2.
  • The total probability of a birth being identical twin boys is:
    P(I and BB) = P(I) √ó P(BB | I) = 1/300 √ó 1/2 = 1/600

For Fraternal Twins (F):

  • The probability of a birth being fraternal is P(F) = 1/125.
  • The sex of each fraternal twin is independent, so the probability of two boys is P(BB | F) = 1/2 √ó 1/2 = 1/4.
  • The total probability of a birth being fraternal twin boys is:
    P(F and BB) = P(F) √ó P(BB | F) = 1/125 √ó 1/4 = 1/500

Step 2: Find the Total Probability of Having Twin Boys

Next, we add the two possibilities together to find the overall probability of any twin birth being two boys.

P(BB) = P(I and BB) + P(F and BB)

P(BB) = 1/600 + 1/500

To add these, we find a common denominator (3000):

P(BB) = 5/3000 + 6/3000 = 11/3000

This means that for every 3000 births, we can expect 11 to be twin boys.

Step 3: Calculate the Conditional Probability

Now we can answer the core question: Given that the twins were boys, what's the probability they were identical?

The formula is: P(Identical | Twin Boys) = P(Identical and Twin Boys) / P(Twin Boys)

We plug in our calculated values:

P(I | BB) = (1/600) / (11/3000)

P(I | BB) = 1/600 √ó 3000/11

P(I | BB) = 3000 / 6600 = 5/11

Conclusion

Knowing that Elvis had a twin brother dramatically increases the likelihood of him being an identical twin. The probability jumps from a baseline of 1/300 (about 0.3%) to a much more substantial 5/11 (about 45.5%). This is because a pair of twin boys is slightly more likely to be fraternal (6/3000) than identical (5/3000), but the chances are very close.
